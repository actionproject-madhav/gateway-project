The Impact of My Dual-Role Internship Experience

My unique dual role as both a Software Engineering and Business Analytics Intern at Massey Services Inc. provided an unprecedented opportunity to bridge the gap between technical development and business strategy. This experience fundamentally transformed my understanding of how technology drives business decisions and how data insights shape product development.

Key Transformations:

From a classroom-focused computer science student to a contributor impacting 10,000+ daily users through production systems. I evolved from theoretical knowledge to practical application, debugging critical issues in live systems and architecting solutions that generate real revenue. The dual perspective of both building technology and analyzing its business impact has given me a holistic view that most interns never experience.

On the software engineering side, I resolved critical production bugs affecting thousands of users daily and architected a full-stack customer rewards system that processes over $5,000 in monthly referral revenue. This wasn't just coding‚Äîit was building systems that directly impact business outcomes. Simultaneously, my business analytics role involved analyzing operational datasets across 500+ locations, creating insights that influenced major technology investment decisions worth $5,000+ monthly.

The most powerful aspect was seeing both sides of the equation: building the systems that generate data, then analyzing that data to improve business operations. This dual perspective taught me that successful technology professionals must understand not just how to build, but why they're building and what impact their work has on the organization.

Gateway Fellowship - Breaking Down Financial Barriers

The Gateway Fellowship funding was not just financial support‚Äîit was the bridge between my potential and opportunity. As a rising sophomore at Rollins College, pursuing an unpaid or low-paid internship in Orlando would have meant choosing between gaining valuable experience and meeting basic living expenses.

Honest Truth:

Would I have pursued this internship without the Gateway Fellowship funding? Absolutely not. As an international student from a low income family, managing finances in college is hard for me. Without the gateways funding, I would be limited by my financial barriers in making my summer decision. The funding clearly provided me an amazing support to live on housing at Rollins Campus and I didn't have to worry much about the cost of living, providing me an incredible opportuntiy to fully immerse in the internship experience

The Gateway Fellowship didn't just fund my internship‚Äîit funded my future. This funding allowed me to take risks, ask questions, volunteer for challenging projects, and truly immerse myself in the experience rather than stress about making ends meet.

More importantly, the fellowship connected me with a network of other ambitious students and provided mentorship that extended far beyond financial support. It demonstrated that someone believed in my potential enough to invest in it, which gave me confidence to pursue opportunities I might have otherwise considered out of reach.
